Analysis of Water Quality in Malaysia (2000-2021)

This dataset tracks the proportion of monitored basins classified as clean, slightly polluted, or polluted based on three key parameters:

BOD5

Biochemical Oxygen Demand - measures organic pollution levels

53.6% Improvement

86.11% Clean in 2021

NH3N

Ammoniacal Nitrogen - indicates presence of ammonia from sewage/agricultural runoff

Moderate Improvement

40.28% Clean in 2021

SS

Suspended Solids - measures particles in water affecting clarity

Strong Improvement

88.19% Clean in 2021

Key Findings

  • Overall Improvement: Significant water quality improvement across all parameters from 2000 to 2021
  • Mid-2010s Challenge: Pollution spike between 2013-2018, particularly for BOD5 and NH3N
  • Persistent Issues: NH3N pollution remains higher compared to other parameters (34.03% basins still polluted in 2021)
  • Monitoring Expansion: Number of monitored basins increased from 120 in 2000 to 144 in 2021

Recommendations

Strengthen Monitoring

Continue expanding basin coverage and enforcement of regulations

Target NH3N Reduction

Focus on reducing ammonia through better wastewater treatment

Public Awareness

Educate communities about water conservation and pollution prevention

Infrastructure Investment

Upgrade treatment plants and implement green infrastructure solutions

Our organization has been at the forefront of water quality monitoring for over two decades. We've expanded from 120 basins in 2000 to 144 basins in 2021, providing comprehensive coverage of critical waterways.

Through rigorous data collection and analysis, we've helped identify pollution trends and supported environmental policy decisions that have led to significant improvements in water quality indicators.
